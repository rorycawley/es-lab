## The Strategic Hierarchy

Before stating the Vision, it is worth being precise about what each level of
the strategic hierarchy means and why the levels are distinct. Conflating them
produces documents that feel strategic but cannot guide decisions.

| Level | Question It Answers | Horizon | Changes When |
|-------|---------------------|---------|-------------|
| **Vision** | What does the world look like when we have fully succeeded? | 10–20 years | The organisation's fundamental purpose shifts |
| **Mission** | Why do we exist? What do we do, for whom? | Enduring | Core purpose or constituency changes |
| **Drivers** | What pressures and constraints are shaping us right now? | 1–5 years | The environment, law, or competitive landscape shifts |
| **Goals** | What strategic outcomes are we pursuing in this period? | 3–5 years | Strategy is revised or achieved |
| **Objectives** | What specific, measurable targets will we hit to achieve each goal? | 6–24 months | Targets are met or reset |
| **KPIs** | How do we know, continuously, that we are moving in the right direction? | Ongoing | Measurement approach is refined |

The levels form a cascade. A KPI that cannot be traced to an Objective is
noise. An Objective that cannot be traced to a Goal has no strategic
justification. A Goal that contradicts the Mission should not exist. Decisions
that contradict the Vision should be challenged.

In practice:
- **Vision and Mission** are stable. They constrain everything below them.
- **Drivers** explain why the current strategy is shaped the way it is.
  They change as the environment changes.
- **Goals and Objectives** are reviewed annually. If you hit all your
  objectives, you set new ones — or update the goal.
- **KPIs** are measured continuously. A KPI going in the wrong direction is
  an early warning that an objective is at risk.

## Vision

> **Every company that operates in this jurisdiction stands on a foundation of
> verified, public, and permanent legal fact — and anyone who needs to know
> whether a company is real, who runs it, and where it can be reached can find
> that answer in seconds, with complete confidence.**

This is the world the Registry is building toward. It is not fully achieved on
the day the system goes live. It is achieved when:

- Every registered company has passed a lawful process under the Act before
  it existed in the Register.
- The Register has never been tampered with, and its integrity can be proven
  to any auditor at any point in time.
- Any member of the public, any business, and any court can search the Register
  and trust what they find — including the history of struck-off and dissolved
  companies.
- The Registry's decisions are permanently and immutably recorded, with the
  identity of the actor, the time, and the reason, for as long as those records
  need to exist.
- Compliance obligations (annual returns, director notifications, address
  changes) are met routinely, keeping the Register accurate after registration,
  not just at the moment of registration.

### What the Vision Is Not

The Vision is not a technology aspiration. "Being event-sourced" or "having
a CQRS architecture" are implementation choices that serve the Vision — they
are not the Vision itself. Architecture decisions are justified by how they
serve this statement, not the other way around.

The Vision is not a service-level target. "99.9% availability" is a KPI that
supports the Vision. It is not the Vision.

The Vision is the answer to the question a CEO asks in year fifteen: did we
build what we set out to build? Have we created the institutional trust we
promised?

## Mission

### Relationship to Vision

The **Vision** describes the world we are building toward — the long-term
outcome if the Registry succeeds over decades.

The **Mission** describes what the Registry does every day to move toward that
Vision. It is the organisation's reason for existing. It answers: *why do we
exist, what do we do, and for whom?*

A Mission is not a goal. Goals change. The Mission is stable — it is the
constant that gives goals their context. If a proposed goal cannot be justified
by the Mission, it does not belong here.

### Registry Mission

> **We execute the Companies Registration Act.**
>
> We register companies, granting them legal existence under the Act. We
> maintain the Register as the authoritative, permanent, and tamper-evident
> record of every company registered in this jurisdiction. We examine
> applications, enforce compliance obligations, and protect the integrity of
> the Register — so that the public, businesses, and the courts have a single
> source of truth they can rely on without qualification or doubt.

### What This Means in Practice

**We register companies.** Not just as an administrative act — as a legal act.
A company exists because we recorded the fact of its registration. That fact
is permanent. We are the institution that creates it, and we are responsible
for ensuring it was created through a lawful process.

**We maintain the Register.** The Register is not a database table. It is the
legal record of company existence in this jurisdiction. Maintaining it means
keeping it accurate after registration, not just at the moment of registration.
Director changes, address changes, annual returns, strike-offs, and
dissolutions all update the record. Keeping the Register accurate is an
ongoing obligation, not a one-time action.

**We enforce compliance.** Companies that do not file annual returns or notify
changes undermine the reliability of the Register for everyone who depends on
it. Enforcement is not punitive — it is the mechanism by which the Register
remains trustworthy.

**We protect the integrity of the record.** No event is ever altered or
deleted. Every decision is permanently recorded with the identity of the actor,
the timestamp, and the reason. The audit trail is not a compliance feature — it
is what makes the Register trustworthy. A Register that could be altered in
secret is not a Register.

**We serve the public.** The Register is open to public inspection by law. Any
person who needs to verify that a company is real, who its directors are, or
where it can be reached must be able to find that information. The Register
exists to support commerce, legal proceedings, and public accountability.

### Who We Serve

| Constituency | What They Need From Us |
|---|---|
| Applicants | A clear, fair process to register a company and receive confirmation of legal existence |
| Registered companies | The ability to maintain accurate particulars and meet their ongoing obligations |
| The public | An open, searchable, accurate record of company existence and standing |
| The courts | An authoritative source of truth for company existence, directors, and registered office |
| The State | A permanent, auditable record of every registration decision made under the Act |
| External auditors | A human-readable, inspectable audit trail of Registry decisions |

### Scope of the Mission

The Mission is bounded by the Act. The Registry does not perform
identity-proofing ceremonies — it consumes trusted verification outcomes. It
does not operate a payment gateway — it instructs one. It does not adjudicate
commercial disputes — it records facts.

Where the Act is silent or reserves a matter for future amendment, the Registry
operates within that silence and does not expand its mandate unilaterally.
Parked concerns are named, visible, and deferred — not silently absorbed.
